# PAGEINFO.MD

## Page purpose
Create a high-converting Services page for a fulfillment/logistics company.
The page should explain how the service works, what services are offered, and why the company is a reliable operational partner.

Primary goal:
- make the service feel clear, scalable, and easy to start
- reduce uncertainty around onboarding and daily operations
- encourage the visitor to request a quote or contact the company

## Target audience
- e-commerce brands
- marketplace sellers
- businesses that want to outsource fulfillment, storage, shipping, and returns
- small and mid-sized companies that need logistics without building their own warehouse team

## Tone of voice
- commercial
- clear
- confident
- practical
- modern
- no fluff
- no exaggerated hype
- no corporate bureaucracy

## Important instruction
Use this file as a structured brief, not as final copy.

Do NOT copy the reference wording literally.
Rewrite everything in fresh, natural marketing language.
You may expand the ideas, improve structure, add transitions, subheadings, helper text, and CTA copy.

## Do not invent
Do not invent:
- fake case studies
- unsupported guarantees
- exact numbers that are not provided here
- extra partner claims
- integrations or operational features that are not mentioned here
- legal / customs promises that may be inaccurate

If information is missing, use neutral wording instead of making up specifics.

## Non-negotiable page structure
The page must include these main sections:
1. Our Approach
2. Our Services
3. Our Partners

You may also add:
- a short intro paragraph inside a section
- small supporting text between blocks
- a compact CTA near the end
- a “Getting started” / “Next steps” block

## Section 1 — Our Approach

### Goal of this section
Show that the company has a clear, transparent, scalable workflow.
This section should reduce fear and make the process feel manageable.

### Required sub-block A — 4 principle cards
Create 4 concise cards that explain the operating model.

Cards must cover:
1. Analyze
   - assess product range
   - assess seasonality
   - assess average order value
   - assess customer geography
   - explain expected savings and delivery improvements
   - mention realistic start volume

2. Integration & Onboarding
   - connect store / API
   - sync inventory / balances
   - provide access to account, history, analytics
   - make setup feel simple even for companies without in-house IT

3. Operational Routine
   - orders flow automatically from the website
   - warehouse team picks, packs, and hands over to courier
   - client receives shipped status and tracking
   - end-of-day reporting is available
   - client involvement is minimal except for restocking

4. Scaling & Optimization
   - capacity for peak periods
   - flexible team / reserve capacity
   - monthly review of key logistics metrics
   - constant optimization of cost and speed
   - logistics should feel like a business advantage, not a bottleneck

### Required sub-block B — Process timeline
Create a separate timeline-style block that presents the workflow as chronological onboarding steps.

This is NOT a duplicate of the cards above.
The cards explain the model.
The timeline explains how the collaboration starts and develops over time.

Required steps:
1. Audit and Trial Period (Week 1)
2. Integration and Onboarding (Week 2)
3. Operational Routine (Day 1 and beyond)
4. Scaling and Optimization (Month 3+)

For each step include:
- short heading
- short explanatory paragraph
- practical outcome / result

### Optional supporting block
Add a compact “How to get started” or “Next steps” block.

Reference ideas:
- contact via WhatsApp / Telegram / website form
- send product photos and average monthly volume
- receive quote quickly
- start with first batch
- low-friction onboarding
- avoid punitive long-term commitment wording unless confirmed elsewhere

## Section 2 — Our Services

### Goal of this section
Present the company not as a warehouse landlord, but as a full operational partner.

### Intro direction
The section should communicate:
- this is not just storage
- this is a full post-sale operations system
- services should feel practical and outcome-driven

### Required compact capability list
Include these service directions in some form:
1. Organizing Product Collection
2. Receiving Product
3. Safe Storage
4. Picking and Packing Online Orders
5. Preparing and Packing Product (FBO)
6. Picking and Packing FBS/DBS
7. Delivery
8. Processing Returns

This can be presented as:
- icon grid
- checklist
- compact cards
- service menu

### Required detailed service cards
Feature these 5 services as expanded cards or content blocks:

1. Turnkey Fulfillment
Key ideas:
- collection or intake of goods
- completeness check
- defect photo documentation
- storage
- picking and packing
- quality / weight check
- courier handoff
- real-time statuses and tracking
- suitable for stores from small to large order volume

2. Cross-border Logistics
Key ideas:
- Armenia to international destinations and back
- export support
- customs support
- shipment consolidation
- import support for purchases from US / Germany
- should sound practical and credible, not overly broad

3. White-label & Brand Experience
Key ideas:
- branded packaging
- inserts / thank-you cards / samples / coupons
- packaging as part of customer experience
- support brand perception and loyalty

4. Returns Management
Key ideas:
- returns intake
- condition check
- photo documentation
- return reason sorting
- follow-up action based on policy
- faster reverse logistics

5. Custom Integrations
Key ideas:
- Shopify
- WooCommerce
- 1C
- Bitrix
- MySklad
- custom CMS
- automatic order import
- inventory sync
- tracking number automation
- reduce manual work and human error

Important:
The 8-item capability list and the 5 detailed cards are two different layers of the same section.
Do not merge them into one confusing block.
