Common client questions

Practical questions teams ask before outsourcing logistics.

The point of this section is to make the service easier to evaluate: how pricing is formed, how responsibility is divided, how dispatch timing is defined, and what stays under your control.

How is pricing structured, and will we see the calculation in advance?

Pricing is usually built around the actual operating blocks involved: receiving, storage, product operations, order assembly, packaging, dispatch, and returns. Before launch, we outline what is included, what affects cost, and which tasks need separate calculation so the commercial model is visible before work begins.

Is the service suitable only for large brands, or can a smaller business start too?

The service is not limited to high-volume operators. If a smaller brand already has recurring orders, marketplace requirements, or warehouse work that is becoming unstable, the launch scope can be kept lighter at the start and expanded as order count and channel complexity grow.

Who is responsible if a shipment is delayed, lost, or needs replacement?

Responsibility depends on the stage of the process. We are accountable for the handling that sits inside the warehouse contour, while carrier-side events are tracked and escalated through the agreed workflow. Exact liability terms are fixed in the working agreement, so the client is not left reconstructing the issue manually.

Do we keep control over our stock, and can goods be withdrawn if needed?

Yes. The stock remains yours, and access, withdrawals, returns, or transfer to another operator are handled through a defined process. The goal is operational control with transparency, not a warehouse model that locks the client into unclear conditions.

How quickly are orders dispatched, and how is the service standard agreed?

Dispatch timing is agreed during setup based on channel rules, cut-off times, SKU mix, and the real order profile. Once that standard is defined, execution is tracked against it, and exceptions are handled as an operating issue rather than disappearing into informal communication.

Can you handle fragile items or products with special packing rules?

Often yes, provided the product category and handling requirements are clear in advance. Fragile goods, extra packaging, labeling steps, or specific assembly sequences are reviewed before launch so the handling method matches the operational risk.

Can the operation work with common platforms and custom workflows?

We can work with common platforms and also review custom workflows where technically practical. If the current technical setup is limited, the launch can begin with a simpler data handoff and move toward deeper integration once the operating process is stable.
