## FAQ Section

### Goal of this section
Create an FAQ block that removes hesitation, answers practical objections, and helps the visitor feel safe starting with the service.

This section should not feel like generic filler.
It must address the real questions a potential client may hesitate to ask directly, especially about pricing, minimum volume, responsibility, speed, integrations, and operational trust.

### Core idea
The FAQ should answer uncomfortable but decision-critical questions in a clear, confident, commercially credible way.

The section should:
- reduce friction before contact
- remove fear of hidden conditions
- clarify how cooperation works in practice
- reinforce trust through transparency and operational confidence

### Tone of voice
- clear
- direct
- reassuring
- practical
- commercially confident
- no vague corporate wording
- no defensive tone
- no overpromising

### Structure requirements
The FAQ section should contain:
- a strong section heading
- an optional short intro line
- 5 to 8 meaningful questions
- concise but informative answers

The questions should feel natural and realistic, as if they come from actual business concerns.

### What the FAQ should cover
The FAQ should cover the most important client objections and uncertainties, such as:

1. Pricing transparency
   - how pricing is structured
   - whether there are hidden fees
   - whether the client sees the calculation in advance

2. Small business / minimum volume concerns
   - whether the service is suitable for small brands
   - whether there is an entry-level package or flexible start model
   - how scaling works as order volume grows

3. Responsibility and lost shipments
   - who is responsible if the shipment is lost or delayed
   - how claims or replacements are handled
   - how much of the operational burden stays on the client

4. Stock access and control
   - whether the client can access or withdraw their goods
   - whether the stock remains fully under the client’s control
   - whether switching operators is possible

5. Shipping speed / SLA
   - how quickly orders are dispatched
   - whether there is a service-level commitment
   - what happens if the company fails to meet the stated service standard

6. Fragile or special goods
   - whether fragile products can be handled
   - whether special packaging or handling protocols exist
   - how safety and damage prevention are approached

7. Technical integration flexibility
   - whether the company can work with common platforms
   - whether custom systems can also be supported
   - what happens if the client has limited technical infrastructure

### Content guidance
The FAQ should focus on practical concerns, not generic “what is fulfillment?” definitions.

Each answer should:
- address the concern directly
- explain the logic in plain business language
- make the service feel transparent and manageable
- strengthen trust without sounding salesy

Where relevant, answers may mention:
- pricing model principles
- service flexibility
- accountability
- process transparency
- operational safeguards
- realistic onboarding simplicity

### Do not invent
Do not invent:
- exact prices unless they are confirmed elsewhere
- exact SLA numbers unless provided
- compensation policies unless verified
- damage-rate statistics unless verified
- guaranteed technical compatibility with every system
- legal or insurance claims unless confirmed

If exact numbers are missing, use credible neutral wording.

For example:
- “transparent pricing structure”
- “entry-level option for smaller brands”
- “defined dispatch timeframe”
- “support for common platforms and custom workflows where technically possible”

### Writing rules
- avoid long answers
- avoid repeating the same trust message in every item
- make each question distinct
- use natural wording, not robotic FAQ phrasing
- write answers in a way that feels operationally credible
- the section should feel helpful, not defensive

### Presentation guidance
This section can be presented as:
- accordion
- collapsible FAQ list
- two-column Q&A layout
- stacked cards

Accordion format is preferred if the page is already content-heavy.

### Optional intro direction
The intro line may frame the section as:
- answers to common client concerns
- practical questions before starting
- things clients usually ask before outsourcing logistics

Avoid overly comedic or ironic wording unless the whole website tone supports it.

### Important distinction
If the source notes include a “How to get started” / “Next steps” / “What you need to begin” block, treat it as a separate closing section after FAQ, not as part of the FAQ itself.

## Optional follow-up block — Getting Started

### Goal
Add a simple low-friction onboarding block after the FAQ.

### What it may include
- contact via WhatsApp / Telegram / website form
- send product photos and average monthly order volume
- receive a quote
- bring or send the first batch
- mention simple start conditions

### Writing rules
Keep this block short, practical, and action-oriented.
It should feel like a natural next step after objections have been resolved in the FAQ.
